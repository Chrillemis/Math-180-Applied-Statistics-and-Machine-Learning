Introduction to Problem Set¶

Your physicist friend is studying diffraction and has asked you to analyze a new experimental dataset (which can be found in the file diffraction.csv on CatCourses). Specifically, your friend has measured the radiation intensity $Y$ for a number of experimental offsets $X$. According to theory, these should be related through \begin{align} Y = a+b\frac{\textup{sin}(\pi c X)}{\pi c X} + \epsilon, \end{align} where $a$ is the intensity of the background, $b$ is the maximum intensity contribution from diffraction, $c$ is the ratio of the width of the diffraction slit and the wavelength of the light, and $\epsilon$ is a random error term. Your friend claims to know that $c=10$ and wants you to estimate $a$ and $b$ from the data. Being an R wiz, you learn a preliminary model using
